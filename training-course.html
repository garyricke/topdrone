Section 01 — The Build

Professional DIY Obstacles

The DJI Avata 2 is a cinewhoop built for tight spaces. Your obstacles need to match — high visibility, prop-safe, and easy to reposition. Everything here can be built for under $60.

Obstacle Materials Purpose
Air Gates 3/4" PVC + Pool Noodles Thread the needle through doorways and hallways
Slalom Posts 4ft PVC + Concrete bucket base Throttle discipline and weave-path control
Precision Buckets 5-gallon bucket + printed QR Hover accuracy based on NIST standards
Limbo Bars Tension rods or weighted PVC Low-clearance training under desks or racking
LED Strips Battery-powered RGB strips Mark safe paths for low-light corridor training
Pro Tip: Wrap all PVC gates in neon duct tape. The Avata 2's FPV camera reads high-contrast markings far better than dull gray pipe — you'll hit fewer gates and log cleaner data.
Section 02 — The Drills

Four Phase Progression

Don't jump straight into Manual Mode in a warehouse. These four drills build the muscle memory you need — in the right order. Each phase unlocks the next.

Phase 01
The Box Drill
Normal / Sport Mode

Set up four cones in a 10×10 ft square. Fly the full perimeter while keeping the camera locked on a central target object. Feel the Avata 2's drift — it handles unlike any standard GPS drone.

Goal Master yaw-coordinated turns and drift management. This is cinewhoop fundamentals.
Phase 02
The Hallway Slalom
Normal / Sport Mode

Place 3–5 weighted slalom posts down a hallway spaced 5 ft apart. Weave between them without stopping. Beginners pump the throttle — the drone bobs. Your job is perfectly level altitude, every pass.

Goal Throttle discipline. Smooth, constant altitude through every gate at increasing speed.
↕
Verticality & Depth
Phase 03
The Rafter Dive
Mixed Modes

Fly up a stairwell or over a high rafter beam, then drop vertically through a gate positioned on the floor below. This trains signal management and prop wash recovery during high-speed descents.

Goal Steady video feed with zero prop wash wobble on the descent. Clean entry, clean exit.
Phase 04
Gap Mastery
Manual / Acro Mode

Set the gate to its minimum keyhole setting: 1.5×1.5 ft (46×46 cm). Approach from 20 ft in full Manual Mode. Thread the gap, execute a 180° Power Loop, and return through the same gate on the return pass.

Goal Total autonomy. Rapid stick inputs, coordinated power loop, zero assistance from flight controller.
Section 03 — The Curriculum

Four Weeks to
Full Control

A structured four-week progression moves you from GPS-assisted Normal Mode all the way to unassisted Manual/Acro. One daily drill. One measurable success metric.

W1
Week 01 — Normal Mode
Precision & Stability

Daily Drill: The Figure-8. Fly around two gates 10 ft apart. The path must be a perfect figure-eight, no deviation.

Success: 5 laps without altitude varying more than 6 inches.
W2
Week 02 — Sport Mode
Obstacle Course

Daily Drill: The Slalom. Place 4 gates in a zigzag. Fly through them at 50% throttle. Speed comes later — precision comes first.

Success: Clear all 4 gates without touching a pool noodle for 3 consecutive batteries.
W3
Week 03 — Mixed Modes
Verticality & Depth

Daily Drill: The Stairwell/Rafter Dive. Climb over a high obstacle, then drop vertically through a gate at floor level. Manage signal and prop wash.

Success: Steady video feed with no wobble during full descent.
W4
Week 04 — Manual / Acro Mode
Gap Mastery

Daily Drill: The Keyhole. Gate set to 1.5×1.5 ft minimum. Approach from 20 ft in full Manual Mode. Thread it, Power Loop, return through the same gate.

Success: Pass through the gate and complete the 180° Power Loop on 3 of 5 attempts.

Section 04 — Training Log

Track Every
Battery

Progress is invisible without data. Log every flight. Record hits, crashes, and the small adjustments that add up to mastery. Keep it in a notebook or a spreadsheet — just keep it.

Two key variables to track beyond hits/crashes: RC Expo settings (controls stick sensitivity) and lighting conditions. The Avata 2's FPV camera responds to ambient contrast — your best days and worst days often correlate directly to room lighting, not pilot error.

Date Battery # Drill Hits / Crashes Notes
Day 1 1 Figure-8 0 / 0 Felt twitchy in turns; adjusted RC Expo
Day 1 2 Slalom 0 / 2 Clipped a gate; need to lead the turn earlier
Day 2 3 Slalom 4 / 0 Lowered throttle rate 15% — much smoother
Day 3 4 Box Drill 5 / 0 Consistent 5-lap run, altitude steady
Day 4 5 Rafter Dive 2 / 1 Prop wash on descent — need more speed entry

Section 07 — Build Guide

Build One Gate

One gate, sized for the Avata 2, built from off-the-shelf 3⁄4″ Schedule 40 PVC. Master the cuts and glue sequence on this first one — then crank out the rest.

Pipe spec: 3⁄4″ Schedule 40 PVC. The 28″ gate opening gives you a 3× clearance on the Avata 2's prop-tip span — comfortable for building precision at speed, tight enough to demand accuracy.

Other Materials
ItemNotes

Assembly Sequence
01
Cut All Pipe
Mark and cut every piece before gluing anything. Label each piece with tape. Deburr the inside of every cut with a utility knife or sandpaper — rough edges weaken the joint.
02
Dry-Fit the Octagon
Assemble the full ring without cement first. All 8 elbows should sit flat in one plane with no twisting. Use a flat floor as your reference surface. Adjust cut lengths now if needed.
03
Glue the Octagon
Work around the ring in one direction. Prime both surfaces, apply cement, push and twist 90° to seat. You have about 30 seconds to adjust. Hold for 15 seconds. Let the full ring cure 1 hour before handling.
04
Attach the Post T
Glue the gate T-fitting to the center of the bottom side — the two 5.25″ sections flank it. Orient the T branch pointing straight down. This is the post socket — keep it perfectly plumb.
05
Build the Stand
Glue T#1 and T#2 onto the upper and lower post sections. T#1 branch faces down (base center), T#2 branch faces perpendicular to T#1's legs. Glue 2 base legs into each T. Cap all 4 ends.
06
Join Gate to Stand
With both assemblies fully cured, cement the post top into the gate bottom T. Check plumb from two angles before the cement sets. Let sit 2 hours before flying through it.
