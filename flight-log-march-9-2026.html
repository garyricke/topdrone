The Story

Mission Debrief

March 9th, 2026. Gary and Julian met at Memorial Park around noon for the first official flight of the DJI Avata 2. Before a single prop turned, the real work began — FAA drone registration, Remote ID entry, setting up the goggles, pairing the RC3 controller, connecting the DJI Fly app to a phone, and managing it all on a MacBook Pro at a picnic table. Multiple devices, multiple menus, and zero manual. Just Gemini AI fielding question after question.

After wrestling the Remote ID out of the system and getting everything linked, the drone lifted off for the first time — five feet of hover, the motors humming, the goggles active. Both pilots flew on that first battery, but the drone felt sluggish. That was Beginner Mode doing its job a little too well. Getting it disabled took patience and persistence — and once it was off, the Avata 2 was a completely different aircraft. Fast, responsive, aggressive. The difference was night and day.

A calibration issue stuck at 95% nearly ended the day early, but a simple power cycle of both the goggles and the drone simultaneously cleared it. Then the crew packed up and drove to Blackberry Maples Forest Preserve, where Julian flew the trail at full tilt — threading through trees flanking both sides, calling out turns in real time while Gary watched through the phone connected to the goggles. One crash. Turtle Mode. Back in the air. Up to 300 feet. Four videos captured. A first flight worth logging.

What We Nailed

Key Accomplishments

📋
FAA Registration + Remote ID
Created the FAA account, registered the drone, and entered the Remote ID transmitted by the Avata 2 — all from a picnic table using Gemini to navigate the menus.
🔗
Full System Paired
Drone, RC3 controller, DJI goggles, DJI Fly app on phone, and MacBook Pro all linked and operational. A genuinely complex multi-device setup — completed in the field.
🚀
Beginner Mode Disabled
Both pilots flew in Beginner Mode, then figured out how to disable it. The Avata 2 immediately became a different aircraft — significantly faster, more responsive, and far more capable.
🏠
Return to Home Confirmed
On low battery, a red alert in the goggles triggered Return to Home. One button press. The drone came back precisely to the takeoff point. Feature verified and working perfectly.
🌲
Tree Line Flying at the Forest Preserve
Julian flew the forested trail at Blackberry Maples at full speed — weaving between trees on both sides. First real FPV experience in a dynamic, technical environment.
📡
Live FPV Feed Shared
Phone connected to the goggles let both pilots see the same FPV feed. A short cable was the only limitation. Solution: get a longer cord.
🔆
ND Filter Installed
Tested an ND filter mid-session for better motion blur in footage. Successfully installed and re-installed without disrupting flight operations.
🐢
Crash Recovery via Turtle Mode
Julian crashed in the trees. Turtle Mode flipped the drone right-side up. Back in the air in seconds. The drone took the hit with zero damage.
⬆️
300 Feet Altitude
Pushed up to 300 feet — three-quarters of the 400-foot legal ceiling. Wind was noticeable at that altitude. Brought it back down confidently.
🎬
Footage Retrieved — No Special Software
Plugged the Avata 2 into the MacBook via USB. Four videos transferred as clean MP4 files — dropped straight into editing with no conversion, no app, no friction.
🤖
Gemini as Real-Time Manual
Used Gemini AI constantly to navigate setup, troubleshoot calibration, and find answers faster than any physical manual could provide. Game changer.
What We Fought

Challenges

🔍
Finding the Remote ID
The Remote ID isn't labeled clearly in the Avata 2 menus. Bouncing between the goggles, the app, and Gemini — it took real persistence before Julian located the correct number.
🐌
Escaping Beginner Mode
Took a disproportionate amount of time. Setting limits, navigating menus, going back and forth between app and goggles — should have been faster, but now we know exactly where it is.
⚙️
Calibration Stuck at 95%
After swapping batteries the drone would not recalibrate — stuck at 95% every attempt. Tried everything Gemini suggested. Fix: power cycle both the goggles and drone simultaneously. Never happened again.
💾
SD Card Too Slow for 4K
The SD card purchased alongside the drone was flagged as too slow. The Avata 2 has built-in storage so footage was saved — but the card must be replaced. Check minimum write speeds before buying.
⚠️
Flight Warnings at Memorial Park
Repeated flight warnings in the DJI Fly app and goggles at the first location. The cause is not yet understood. Needs research — may relate to proximity to restricted airspace or local rules.
📱
Short Phone Cord for FPV Sharing
The cable connecting the phone to the goggles was too short to comfortably share the view with a second person. A longer cord is on the list.
🔌
Multi-Device Setup Complexity
Five active devices at once — drone, goggles, RC3 controller, phone app, and MacBook — each with their own menus and interdependencies. Confusing at first, but manageable once you know the flow.

Equipment
